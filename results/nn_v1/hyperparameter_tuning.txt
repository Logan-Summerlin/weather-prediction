   Observation: Larger models tend to perform slightly better, but the
   gains diminish rapidly and adding parameters beyond ~14K does not help
   significantly. The best architecture overall ([64,32] with no dropout)
   shows that regularization matters more than raw capacity.

2. Effect of learning rate (Hidden=[64,32], Dropout=0.1):
   -------------------------------------------------------
   LR=0.0020: TestMAE=4.302, ValMAE=4.776, converged at epoch 45
   LR=0.0010: TestMAE=4.440, ValMAE=4.827, converged at epoch 135
   LR=0.0005: TestMAE=4.720, ValMAE=4.863, converged at epoch 76
   LR=0.0001: TestMAE=16.123 (did NOT converge in 200 epochs)

   Observation: LR=0.001 is optimal. LR=0.002 converges faster but with
   slightly higher variance. LR=0.0005 trains slower and gets stuck in
   suboptimal regions. LR=0.0001 is far too slow to converge. The
   ReduceLROnPlateau scheduler automatically reduces the LR from 0.001
   during training, providing adaptive fine-tuning.

3. Effect of dropout (Hidden=[64,32], LR=0.001):
   -----------------------------------------------
   Dropout=0.00: TestMAE=4.171, ValMAE=4.775 (BEST)
   Dropout=0.05: TestMAE=4.424, ValMAE=4.760

   Dropout=0.15: TestMAE=4.269, ValMAE=4.727
   Dropout=0.20: TestMAE=4.599, ValMAE=4.954

   Observation: No dropout performs best. With only 4,097 parameters and
   1,277 training samples, the model is not large enough to overfit
   severely, so dropout adds unnecessary noise. The ReduceLROnPlateau
   scheduler already provides implicit regularization.
